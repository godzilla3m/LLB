# BNSS-I Comprehensive Cheat Sheet

| Sr No. | Topic | Sections | Short Explanation | Examples |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **UNIT I** | **Introduction** |  |  |  |
| 1 | **History, Object, Scope & Extent** | 1–5 | BNSS 2023 replaces CrPC; applies to all of India; focuses on justice over mere procedure. | **Prateek** notes that a crime in **Indore** is now governed by BNSS instead of the 1973 Code. |
| 2 | **Definitions & Constitution of Courts** | 6–20 | Defines terms (Bail, Cognizable) and sets up the hierarchy (Sessions, JMIC, Executive). | **Krishna** is appointed as a Judicial Magistrate in **Lucknow** to preside over a new criminal court. |
| 3 | **Powers of Courts** | 21–29 | Specifies what sentences (fine, life, death) each level of court can legally pass. | **Kanishth**, as a Sessions Judge in **Jaipur**, passes a life sentence, which is within his legal power. |
| 4 | **Superior Officers & Aid** | 30–34 | Superior police have same powers as OCs; public must help Magistrates/Police when asked. | **Raghavendra** helps a police officer in **Bhopal** catch a runaway thief as required by Section 33\. |
| **UNIT II** | **Pre-Trial Procedure** |  |  |  |
| 5 | **Arrest of Persons** | 35–62 | Rules for arrest with/without warrant, protection of women, and rights of the accused. | **Laukendra** is arrested in **Patna**; the police must inform him of his right to a lawyer immediately. |
| 6 | **Summons & Warrants** | 63–81 | Methods to make a person appear; Summons (paper) vs. Warrant (arrest order). | A court in **Delhi** sends a summons to **Saurabh** to testify as a witness in a local theft case. |
| 7 | **Proclamation & Attachment** | 82–93 | If a person hides, the court declares them a "proclaimed offender" and can take their property. | **Satyam** flees **Varanasi** to avoid court; the Judge orders his house attached to force him to appear. |
| 8 | **Production of Things** | 94–106 | Process to get documents or items (Summons to produce or Search Warrants). | The police suspect **Sachkeerat** has a stolen laptop in **Amritsar** and get a search warrant to find it. |
| 9 | **Seizure & Forfeiture** | 107–124 | Police power to seize property related to crimes and the court's power to forfeit it. | In **Mumbai**, the court orders the forfeiture of a car used by **Prateek** to transport illegal goods. |
| 10 | **Preventive Action (Police)** | 168–172 | Police can act to prevent a crime before it happens (arresting to prevent a cognizable offense). | **Krishna** (a police officer) in **Pune** detains a suspect to prevent a planned arson attack. |
| 11 | **Investigation Powers** | 173–196 | Filing FIR (including e-FIR), collecting evidence, and witness statements (Sec 161 CrPC equiv). | **Kanishth** files an e-FIR in **Bangalore** for a stolen phone, and the police start a formal investigation. |
| **UNIT III** | **Inquiries Procedure** |  |  |  |
| 12 | **Jurisdiction** | 197–209 | Determines which specific court has the legal right to try a case based on location. | Since the fraud happened in **Hyderabad**, **Raghavendra**'s case is heard there and not in **Kolkata**. |
| 13 | **Initiation of Proceedings** | 210–222 | How a Magistrate takes "Cognizance" of an offense (Police report or private complaint). | A Magistrate in **Surat** takes cognizance after **Laukendra** (police) submits a charge sheet. |
| 14 | **Complaints to Magistrate** | 223–226 | Procedure for private citizens to file a case directly with a Judge. | **Saurabh** files a private complaint in **Jodhpur** after the police refuse to register his FIR. |
| 15 | **Commencement (Trial)** | 227–233 | Issuing the "Process" (Summons/Warrant) to the accused to officially begin the trial. | The Magistrate in **Nagpur** finds enough evidence against **Satyam** and issues a summons to start the trial. |
| **UNIT IV** | **Public Order & Alimony** |  |  |  |
| 16 | **Peace & Good Behaviour** | 125–143 | Bonds taken from people likely to disturb the peace or from habitual offenders. | **Sachkeerat** is asked to sign a bond in **Chandigarh** to ensure he doesn't fight with neighbors again. |
| 17 | **Maintenance (Alimony)** | 144–147 | Support for wives, kids, and parents who can't maintain themselves (Old Sec 125). | In **Shimla**, a court orders **Prateek** to pay monthly maintenance to his dependent parents. |
| 18 | **Public Order/Tranquility** | 148–167 | Handling unlawful assemblies and public nuisances (Old Sec 144). | To stop a violent protest in **Ranchi**, the Magistrate prohibits the gathering of crowds in the area. |
| 19 | **Preventive Action (Police)** | 168–172 | *Repeated in syllabus*: Focuses on police duty to prevent injury to public property. | **Krishna** (police) prevents **Kanishth** from damaging a public park in **Guwahati** during a protest. |
